SMS Messaging Program

Last updated: 07/22/2026

This page documents the text-messaging program Data Thaw operates on behalf of local service businesses, including how consent is obtained, what messages are sent, and how recipients opt out. It exists so that customers, client businesses, and mobile carriers can review our messaging practices in one place.

Program and Message Types

Program name: Data Thaw Appointment & Reactivation Messaging.

Messages fall into two distinct categories, each governed by its own separate consent (see How Consent Is Obtained):

Transactional / service messages

  • Appointment reminders and scheduling confirmations;
  • Follow-ups on an open quote, estimate, or service request the recipient initiated.

Promotional / marketing messages (separate, optional opt-in)

  • Occasional offers, discounts, or “we miss you” reactivation messages from a business the recipient is an existing customer or prior lead of.

A recipient who opts in only to service messages will never receive promotional messages. Promotional messages are sent only to recipients who have separately opted in to them.

Who Sends These Messages

Messages are sent on behalf of, and under the business name of, the local service business that the recipient has an existing relationship with — for example a plumbing, HVAC, dental, or home-services company. Data Thaw operates the messaging platform for that business; the business is the sender of record and is identified by name in every message.

Data Thaw does not purchase, rent, sell, or share contact lists. We only message contacts supplied by the business that already has a relationship with them, and only where that business has obtained consent to send text messages.

How Consent Is Obtained

Consent is collected by the service business at the point the customer provides their mobile number — on that business's own intake or contact form, quote or estimate request, online appointment booking, or at the point of sale — using two separate, independent checkboxes, neither of which is pre-selected:

  • Service messages (checkbox 1): the customer affirmatively agrees to receive transactional appointment and service texts.
  • Promotional messages (checkbox 2 — optional): the customer separately and affirmatively agrees to receive marketing and reactivation offers. This box is optional, is collected independently of the service checkbox, and is never a condition of making a purchase or of receiving service messages.

Marketing consent is never bundled with the service consent or with any other agreement, and neither box is pre-checked.

Required consent disclosures

Every business we send on behalf of is required to display these two separate disclosures, each next to its own checkbox, with its own name substituted:

Message frequency varies. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out or HELP for help. See our Privacy Policy and Terms.

2. Promotional / marketing consent — optional and separate

Optional. By checking this box, you also agree to receive promotional and reactivation text messages (offers and occasional deals) from [Business Name]. Consent to marketing messages is not required to receive service messages or to make a purchase. Message frequency varies. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out or HELP for help. See our Privacy Policy and Terms.

Message Frequency and Rates

Message frequency varies, typically 1–5 messages per inquiry, appointment, or campaign. Message and data rates may apply. Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

Opting Out and Getting Help

  • To opt out: reply STOP, END, CANCEL, UNSUBSCRIBE, or QUIT to any message. You will receive one confirmation message and then no further messages.
  • To resubscribe: reply START.
  • For help: reply HELP or INFO, email support@datathaw.com, or call 803-820-0608.

Opt-out requests are honored immediately and apply to the business that sent the message.

Mobile Information and Privacy

No mobile information will be shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Text messaging originator opt-in data and consent are excluded from all information sharing; this information will not be shared with, or sold to, any third parties.
